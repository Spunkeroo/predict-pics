Best Camera Bags of 2026

Your camera bag is the most important accessory you own — it protects thousands of dollars of gear and determines how comfortably you carry it. After testing 20 bags across hiking trails, airports, and city streets, here are the best camera bags for every use case.

Best Camera Backpacks

Peak Design Everyday Backpack V3 30L ($300) — Best overall. The V3 improves on the already-excellent V2 with better back panel ventilation and a more intuitive FlexFold divider system. Side access lets you grab your camera without removing the pack. Fits a 16-inch laptop. Weatherproof recycled nylon shell. The design is sleek enough for office use, rugged enough for trails.

Lowepro ProTactic BP 450 AW III ($250) — Best for heavy gear. Four access points (top, back, sides) and a modular SlipLock attachment system let you customize external attachment points. Fits 2 bodies + 6-8 lenses. Built-in all-weather cover. Heavily padded shoulder straps and waist belt distribute weight for all-day comfort.

Shimoda Explore V2 35L ($280) — Best for hiking. Adventure-focused design with a real hiking suspension system (hip belt, sternum strap, load lifters). Core Unit camera inserts are removable — convert to a full hiking pack when you want. Excellent ventilation. Holds a camera, 3-4 lenses, tripod, water bladder, and hiking essentials.

Best Shoulder/Messenger Bags

Think Tank Retrospective 30 V2.0 ($180) — Classic messenger design that does not look like a camera bag (theft deterrent). Silent velcro closures for street photography. Holds 1 body + 3-4 lenses. Premium build quality with lifetime warranty.

ONA Bowery ($150) — The most stylish camera bag on the market. Waxed canvas and leather construction ages beautifully. Compact size holds 1 body + 2 lenses. Perfect for street photography with a mirrorless kit.

Best Sling Bags

Peak Design Everyday Sling 6L ($100) — The best grab-and-go bag. Holds 1 body with a medium zoom lens attached plus an extra lens or flash. Cross-body sling design rotates to front for quick access. Weatherproof and surprisingly spacious for its compact size.

Best Travel Camera Bags

Nomatic McKinnon Camera Pack 35L ($350) — Designed with photographer Peter McKinnon. Airline carry-on compliant. Dedicated camera compartment below, clothes compartment above, laptop sleeve in back. The ultimate one-bag travel solution for photographers. TSA-friendly design opens flat for security checkpoints.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best camera bag brand?

Peak Design, Lowepro, Think Tank, and Shimoda are the top camera bag brands. Peak Design offers the best design and versatility. Lowepro and Think Tank offer the best protection. Shimoda is best for outdoor adventures.

How big of a camera bag do I need?

For a mirrorless body plus 2-3 lenses, a 15-20L bag is sufficient. For a full professional kit (2 bodies, 5-6 lenses), you need 25-35L. Always list your gear before choosing a size.

Are camera backpacks allowed as carry-on?

Most camera backpacks under 22x14x9 inches qualify as airline carry-on. The Nomatic McKinnon and Peak Design Everyday Backpack are specifically designed to meet carry-on requirements.

How do I protect my camera gear when traveling?

Use a padded camera bag with adjustable dividers, wrap lenses in microfiber cloths, keep camera bodies with lenses attached to minimize dust entry, and always carry gear in your carry-on luggage — never check it.

Is a camera bag worth it vs a regular backpack?

Yes. Camera bags have padded dividers that prevent lens-to-lens contact, weather sealing to protect against rain, and quick-access designs that let you grab your camera without removing the bag. A regular backpack risks damage from gear shifting and bumping.
